03Reading the card

  • Unmentioned is the primary leakage-reduced relational-generalization indicator — it keeps the description intact and selects queries whose text does not name the answer. It is not "true" generalization: semantic or paraphrased answer evidence may remain; only exact-name mention is excluded.
  • Natural aggregate MRR (24.08) must not be cited as relational generalization — it substantially reflects answer-mention reading (mentioned 44.82 vs unmentioned 10.89).
  • JA is the generalization bottleneck: lowest on every view (unmentioned 3.57), the clearest target for later multilingual work.
  • FR and ES carry the strongest relational signal (unmentioned 14.20 / 12.81).
  • Masked below missing for EN (3.45 vs 6.30): alias-masking deletes legitimate context along with names, dropping below the name-only floor. It is a stress diagnostic, not a clean generalization measure.
