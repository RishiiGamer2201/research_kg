Figure: Detailed system diagram for DBP-5L-Ind. (a) The benchmark holds out entities rather than merely triples, creating the multilingual inductive setting. (b) Every entity is represented as text, with descriptions enriched from names, KG neighborhoods, Wikipedia/DBpedia abstracts, and a small disclosed LLM back-fill. (c) The model is a shared BGE-M3 bi-encoder with frozen backbone weights and trainable LoRA adapters; training combines in-batch negatives with per-epoch hard-negative mining and optimizes CRR, a smooth reciprocal-rank surrogate. (d) Evaluation ranks unseen tails by encoding their descriptions at inference time and filters all known true facts, including support edges. (e) The corrected full model reaches 26.69% MRR, with most gain attributed to text quality and context length. (f) Remaining gates are seed 777, anchor-aware evaluation, external mBERT baselines, and paired significance tests.
