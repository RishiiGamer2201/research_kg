CRR objective: differentiable reciprocal rank
ℒCRR = log ( 1 + ∑j ≠ y σ( (sj − sy − ρ) / τc ) )
Smooth upper bound on −log RR: each negative j contributes only if it scores within margin ρ of the gold y. Pressure ρ = 0.1, temperature τc = 0.1; exponent clamped to [−80, 80] for stability. First use of CRR beyond English transductive KGC; synergistic with hard negatives (CRR alone ≈ InfoNCE; CRR + HN is what wins).

2 · Rank within language

Query d(h) [SEP] r is scored against the same-language candidate pool (standard mKGC protocol; cross-lingual pool also reported). Tail prediction (h, r, ?).

3 · Filtered metrics

All known true tails removed from the ranking: train ∪ valid ∪ test ∪ support facts. Eval max_length auto-matched to training. MRR, Hits@1/3/10.

(f)The self-healing loop: the graph's structure audits and repairs its own textnew headline direction · evidence in hand
Suspects: descriptions

Entity text, increasingly AI-generated. In our KB: ~70% of LLM back-fill fabricated, concentrated in low-resource EL and JA.

Witness: structure

The entity's own edges, serialized: "directed Unforgiven; won Academy Award". Independent of the text, reviewed fact by fact.

Consistency check

Encode description and structure with BGE-M3, low cosine flags contamination. Zero-shot, style-invariant. ROC-AUC 0.995, F1 0.988; catches 57% of real fabrications with minimal structure.

Quarantine + re-source

Flagged text is removed. Native-language Wikipedia recovered 45%; the rest fall back to the plain, verified name.

Measured repair

Cleaned KB improves completion: +1.95 MRR on Japanese, +0.25 overall. Next: LLM factual-QA grounding gains, largest for low-resource languages.

Why this is the headline: stylometric detection of machine text is documented to struggle (WETBench), while nobody audits a KB's own text against its own structure. We found the phenomenon in the wild, can detect it without training, and have already measured the downstream repair. mRAKL constructs KGs, we clean them; KG error detection finds bad edges, we find fabricated text.

Figure 1: Overview of the proposed system. (a) DBP-5L-Ind, the first entity-disjoint inductive split of DBP-5L: 20% of entities per language are held out entirely from training; evaluation triples touch at least one unseen entity, with ≤5 support edges per unseen entity reserved for context and filtering. (b) A four-stage enrichment cascade gives every entity a textual description; test-entity descriptions never use held-out edges. (c) A single frozen BGE-M3 tower with rank-16 LoRA adapters encodes queries and candidates; training combines in-batch negatives with per-epoch-refreshed mined hard negatives under the CRR loss, a smooth upper bound on reciprocal rank. (d) At inference, unseen entities are represented purely by their encoded text and ranked against cached same-language candidates with standard filtering. (e) The full model reaches 26.51% MRR (3-seed mean, standard deviation 0.31), an 18.3-fold improvement over zero-shot, with the ablation attributing most of the gain to description quality and usable context length. Whiskers show plus or minus one standard deviation across seeds 42, 123, and 777. The improvement over the pre-enrichment baseline is significant (paired bootstrap and Wilcoxon, both p below 0.001). (f) The new headline direction: entity descriptions (the suspects) are checked against the entity's own graph neighbourhood (the witness) by a zero-shot consistency judge; flagged text is quarantined and re-sourced from verified channels. Found in the wild: about 70% of LLM back-filled descriptions were confident fabrications, concentrated in the low-resource languages; repairing them improved Japanese completion by 1.95 MRR, with LLM grounding evaluation as the planned payoff.
